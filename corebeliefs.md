Our Core beliefs:
AI Transformation is about people not technology 
* It’s process change in disguise
* Successful implementation only happens after process mastery and alignment
* There is a scourge of sales tactics and snake oil AI consultants who claim you’re only one template away from a business that’s completely on autopilot. They love the gratification of the easy up-front sale, but have NONE of the appetite needed to actually be a partner to implement that solution and ensure it’s the right fit for your organization and adopted.
Adoption is the most important KPI
* You can’t even begin to assess the impact of a digital transformation unless orgs adopt it and use it in their flow of work. 
* Digital transformations fail, because they’re are often top-down mandates. Successful change is almost always employee-led. 
* The Wisdom Of Crowds states that diverse opinions, independence, and decentralization make collective decisions more accurate and innovative
AI should remove work, not add it 
* Some AI solutions make the mistake of adding a layer of maintenance or process that ineffectively complicates a process
* Ultimately AI solutions are more effective when they remove steps in a process vs. augment how they’re completed. People will continue to perform tasks in a counterproductive manor because it’s habit rather than learn something new. 
* Wow moments and excitement around adoption come when AI solutions create ‘high speed’ rail systems that remove time-consuming and repetitive tasks rather than speed them up. 
* The goal is not more software. It's less busywork and reclaimed attention. You measure success by what human behavior disappears, not by what the system does. If a daily, hated task is gone, that's a win.
Value has to be felt, not explained. 
* If the value needs explaining, adoption will be slow. 
* You believe AI solutions must create immediate, undeniable relief — the kind where people "would fight to keep it" rather than needing a deck to understand why it matters.
* You must align on what success is. It’s much easier to say time is valuable than actually invest money in getting it back. Organizations must be clear on the quantitative outcomes as well as what the value of their time back is and where it could be invested to advance the business.
Not Everything Needs AI
* FOMO Content and snake oil peddlers is ruining all the fun in AI transformation. They claim you’re only one template away from a business that’s completely on autopilot. The truth is that A) AI transformation is much less glamorous and much more difficult to do effectively, and B) Not every process needs to be automated or needs to not be human.
AI doesn't replace human potential. It finally gives it room to breathe.
* Talented people are spending the majority of their time working beneath their potential — repetitive tasks, administrative drag, low-value coordination. AI isn't here to take over; it's here to compress or eliminate that layer so that the uniquely human stuff — judgment, creativity, relationships, courage, vision — finally gets the time and space it deserves.
