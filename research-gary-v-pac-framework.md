# Research: Gary Vaynerchuk's PAC Framework & "Veditors" Concept

## Executive Summary

The **PAC framework** (Platforms, Algorithms, Culture) is a well-documented, central pillar of Gary Vaynerchuk's modern marketing philosophy. It was publicly formalized in early 2023, heavily promoted through his social channels, and codified in his May 2024 book *Day Trading Attention*. PAC is one component of a broader "Modern Advertising Framework" that also includes Cohort Development, SOC (Strategic Organic Content), PCS (Post-Creative Strategy), and Amplification.

The term **"Veditors" (Views Editors)** did not surface in any web-indexed source — not in Gary Vee's published content, VaynerMedia materials, book summaries, social posts, podcast transcripts, industry commentary, or hiring pages. This term may be: (a) very recent and not yet indexed, (b) used informally in speeches/videos not yet transcribed, (c) from a source outside the Gary Vee ecosystem, or (d) a misremembering of a related concept. The closest related concept is his team's emphasis on video editors who optimize content for views/retention on specific platforms — a role he does staff heavily but hasn't branded with a specific term in indexed content.

## 1. The PAC Framework — Full Breakdown

### What PAC Stands For

**P** = Platforms
**A** = Algorithms
**C** = Culture

Sometimes shortened to just "Platforms and Culture" (P&C), with algorithms folded into the platform component.

### Origin & Timeline

- **Early 2023**: Gary Vee began publicly promoting the PAC acronym. A LinkedIn post (activity ID 7042220281439903744) introduced it: "Here's a game-changing framework for you: PAC. It stands for Platforms & ..."
- **April 2023**: TikTok video telling followers to "get used to this term" — "If you're a big brand, a small business owner, or a content creator, 'platforms and culture' needs to be your new religion."
- **May 2023**: Follow-up LinkedIn post: "P.A.C. understand it and watch what happens to the creative you produce"
- **2023 blog post** on garyvaynerchuk.com: "How to Make Content in 2023" — PAC presented as the defining framework for content success
- **May 2024**: *Day Trading Attention* published — PAC formalized as step 2 of the "Modern Advertising Framework"
- **2024-2025**: Continued evangelization at conferences (POSSIBLE 2024 keynote), podcasts, and social content

### The Three Components — Detail

#### Platforms
- Understand the specific features, formats, and mechanics of each platform
- Facebook Reels behave differently than TikTok; Instagram carousels differ from LinkedIn posts
- Inventory all platform features; track new feature tests continuously
- A greenscreen video of a property listing gets "5-10x more views than a selfie video with the same listing"
- Be a **practitioner**, not a headline reader — executives must personally use platforms to understand them
- VaynerMedia employs **100+ specialists** focused on platform algorithms, cultural trends, and content performance

#### Algorithms
- The "TikTokification of social media" — platforms shifting from follower-based (social graph) to interest-based (interest graph) distribution
- AI algorithms now reach people with high propensity interest regardless of whether they follow the creator
- A complete stranger with zero followers can outperform established accounts on their first post
- Gary's approach: trial-and-error posting, analyzing performance, building theories on why content organically takes off
- Algorithms reward content quality and engagement over account size

#### Culture
- Grasp the pulse of sports, music, entertainment, food, local politics, memes, trending moments
- "Good content isn't about 'selling' your product or service — good content is about understanding culture and inserting yourself or your brand into the relevant conversations taking place online"
- Subcultures matter: #BookTok, #CorpoTok, #SportsTok — communities form around genres
- Cultural moments are fleeting; content must be timely and relevant
- To dominate: post 12-20 times per day touching on diverse cultural elements

### How PAC Is Applied at VaynerMedia

- PAC is described as "the new requirement for your advertising knowledge"
- Internal teams leverage cultural insights to make content that resonates with consumers
- "Understanding Platforms and Culture (PAC) is the real goldmine. Campaigns are just the commodity."
- Teams conduct platform-specific research including hashtags, formats, and audience trends
- Content is validated organically before paid amplification — by 2025, spending on unvalidated creative is "marketing malpractice"

## 2. The Full Modern Advertising Framework (from *Day Trading Attention*)

PAC is step 2 of a five-part framework:

### Step 1: Cohort Development
- Define specific audience segments with precision — psychographics, not just demographics
- Treat cohorts like an "accordion" — constantly shifting and expanding
- Consider subcultures, life stages, affinities, passions, psychographics, cultural trends
- Develop a **high volume** of distinct cohorts
- Move beyond broad "avatars" to granular consumer groups

### Step 2: PAC (Platforms, Algorithms, Culture)
- See above

### Step 3: SOC (Strategic Organic Content)
- Content designed to build brand relevance and drive sales without feeling promotional
- Hooks capture attention in first three seconds
- Evoke emotion through storytelling
- Ensure native platform formatting (trending audio, platform-specific tools)
- Maintain consistent, high content volume
- Balance value delivery with business asks
- "Over 90% of social media content lacks strategic direction"

### Step 4: PCS (Post-Creative Strategy)
- Listen to actual consumers — conduct interviews beyond surface questions
- Ask about broader experiences and memories
- Gather insights from audience engagement to inform future content
- Read comments, monitor conversations, identify what resonates
- Shift from "boardroom centric" to "consumer centric" thinking

### Step 5: Amplification
- Test organically first, then boost what works with paid media
- Workflow: Test -> Validate -> Amplify
- Only spend money on creatives that have proven organic traction

## 3. "Veditors" (Views Editors) — Research Findings

### Search Results: Not Found

**The term "Veditors" or "Views Editors" does not appear in any indexed source.**

### Closest Related Concepts Found

1. **Team GaryVee Video Editors**: His team of 30+ members across 15 countries produces 200 pieces of content weekly. Video editors "seek through and review hours of footage to find the best possible contextual creative for the platform." 80% of content is post-production — adding stickers, overlays, words to optimize for each platform.

2. **Platform Strategy Producer (PSP)**: A formal role on Team GaryVee — creates "emotional and impactful strategic organic content" including video, clips, photography, social copy, GIFs, streaming stories.

3. **Post Creative Strategist (PCS)**: Another formal role — monitors brand channels, identifies high-impact engagement opportunities, leverages expertise in popular culture and trends.

4. **Creator**: Produces daily social media content with skills in "producing, design, video editing, social media copywriting" — transforms branded content into "a volume of social gold."

5. **Industry trend (2026)**: Video editing is being reframed as "a core growth function, not a support role" — editors now sit "at the intersection of storytelling, platform behavior, and audience psychology." Editing decisions made with retention, engagement, and conversion in mind. This aligns with the spirit of a "views editor" concept even if the specific term isn't used.

### Possible Explanations

- The term may have been coined in a very recent video, podcast, or live stream (late 2025 / early 2026) that hasn't been indexed or transcribed yet
- It could be internal VaynerMedia terminology not yet publicized
- It may originate from a different thought leader or content creator in the space
- It could be a concept Gary discussed in a keynote or private event
